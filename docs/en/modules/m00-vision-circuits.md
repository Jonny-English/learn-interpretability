# M00 Vision Circuits Warm-up

## Goal

Build intuition for three ideas before touching language models: a neuron can align with a recognizable pattern, several neurons can combine into a circuit, and interventions are easier to reason about once the circuit is visible.

## Core reference

- [Zoom In: An Introduction to Circuits](https://distill.pub/2020/circuits/zoom-in/)

## What to look for

- Why a single neuron visualization can already feel interpretable in a vision model.
- How multiple edge detectors compose into a curve detector.
- Why this visual story is useful, but insufficient, for language-model interpretability.

## Notebook and artifacts

- Notebook: `notebooks/en/m00_vision_circuits.ipynb`
- Shared artifacts: legacy figures and the shared concept graph
- Extended background: `notebooks/circuits_zoom_in_en.ipynb`

## Takeaway

This module is intentionally concrete and visual. It gives readers a stable mental picture that later modules will challenge and refine once superposition enters the story.
