# Evaluation Rubric

Use this rubric at Week 7 and Week 12. Score yourself with evidence, not with vibes.

## Paper Reading

- Not ready:
  - repeats the headline but cannot point to the strongest evidence
  - confuses the question with the result
- Near ready:
  - can explain the question, method, and main result
  - notices at least one limitation
- Ready:
  - can explain question, method, evidence, limitation, and next experiment in 30 minutes
  - can tell a mentor or peer which figure matters most and why
- Hard evidence:
  - one strong paper reading note
  - one brief that survives follow-up questions

## Experiment Reproduction

- Not ready:
  - can only run notebooks as-is
  - does not know what to vary next
- Near ready:
  - can change one variable and observe an effect
  - keeps partial logs and some baselines
- Ready:
  - can design a small sweep
  - can explain the baseline and what changed
  - can document at least one failure mode cleanly
- Hard evidence:
  - one experiment log with a real sweep
  - one writeup that compares baseline versus variant

## Research Judgment

- Not ready:
  - treats a visualization as proof
  - states speculation as if it were settled
- Near ready:
  - notices ambiguity
  - can list alternative explanations after prompting
- Ready:
  - separates observation, inference, and speculation
  - proposes one minimal next experiment that would reduce ambiguity
- Hard evidence:
  - one artifact critique
  - one memo with a real uncertainty section

## Communication

- Not ready:
  - writes long summaries with no decision
  - hides risk and uncertainty
- Near ready:
  - has structure and can summarize the setup
  - still leaves the judgment call blurry
- Ready:
  - writes a two-page memo with summary, evidence, risks, decision, and next steps
  - can give a short spoken walkthrough without collapsing into jargon
- Hard evidence:
  - one midpoint memo
  - one final memo and one short presentation

## Interpreting the result

You are close to research-ready when:

- no skill remains at "not ready"
- at least three skills are at "ready"
- your portfolio contains evidence for the ready scores

If the writing is weak, do not call yourself ready just because the notebook runs. Most beginners underestimate how important written judgment is in real research environments.
