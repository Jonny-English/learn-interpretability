# P6 Graduation Checklist

Use this file to decide whether the repository can honestly call the outcome `P6-ready` for an ordinary undergraduate.

## Direct answer

- Yes, but only if "finished the course" means finishing the full program rather than only the 12-week bootcamp.
- The bootcamp is the core apprenticeship stage.
- The `P6-ready` claim starts only after the learner also clears the graduation checklist below.

## Expected total effort

- Plan for roughly `180-250` focused hours.
- A common shape is `12` weeks of bootcamp at `8-12` hours per week plus `4-8` more weeks for extension reproductions, dossier polishing, and proposal refinement.
- If the learner starts below the repository's entry assumptions, add the `F00-F03` foundation time on top.

## Minimum completion definition for a P6 claim

Call the result `P6-ready` only if the learner has completed all of the following:

1. The live core: `M00`, `M01`, `M02`, `M04`, and `M05`.
2. The reading-and-critique layer: `M03`, `M06`, `M07`, `M08`, `M09`, and `M10`.
3. At least three extension reproductions:
   one from `X01-X03`, one from `X04-X06`, and `X07`.
4. One independent research sprint ending in a scoped research-task dossier.
5. One two-week capstone proposal with a baseline, budget, risks, and stop condition.

## Required evidence package

The portfolio must also contain all of the following:

- ten or more paper reading notes
- ten or more experiment logs
- six or more short research memos
- two or more artifact, graph, or edit critiques
- one two-page decision memo
- one short presentation or spoken walkthrough
- one full dossier with a baseline, one constrained sweep or ablation, failure analysis, and a next experiment

## Rubric threshold

Do not call the outcome `P6-ready` unless:

- no rubric dimension is still at "not ready"
- all four rubric dimensions reach at least "ready"
- the evidence package above is present

If the learner clears most of the list but misses the rubric threshold or the dossier, call it `P5+`, not `P6-ready`.

## Why this checklist makes the P6 claim plausible

- It forces live reproduction, not only reading.
- It forces artifact critique, not only coding.
- It forces decision memos and next-experiment design, not only summaries.
- It forces breadth across circuits, features, editing, and auditing instead of leaving the learner trapped inside one paper line.

That is enough to make the repository's `P6-ready` claim credible for a serious ordinary undergraduate. It is also strict enough to avoid pretending that notebook completion alone equals `P6`.
