# Capability Arc

## Direct answer first

- Yes: an ordinary undergraduate can plausibly reach the repository's described `P6` level if they finish the path seriously and clear the evidence gates.
- The direct target of the repository is therefore `P6-ready` or early `P6`, not merely `P5`.
- `P7` and `P8` remain useful background context for what comes later, but they are outside the repository's direct completion claim.

## The capability arc from P4 upward

- `P4`: can run notebooks under guidance, restate concepts, and record basic experiments.
- `P5`: can independently reproduce scoped papers, keep clean logs, and write baseline-versus-variant conclusions.
- `P6`: can take a clearly scoped research task, critique evidence, and propose the next experiment.
- `P7`: can connect multiple papers into one direction, define a two-week plan, and manage evidence boundaries plus tooling needs.
- `P8`: can own an interpretability sub-area or tooling line and create stable technical leverage for multiple collaborators over time.

## Which repository layer directly supports which segment

- `F00-F03` mainly serve `Pre-P4 -> P4`.
- `M00-M10`, the 12-week bootcamp, the rubric, the reference outputs, and the capstone flow build the core apprenticeship segment of the `P6-ready` path.
- at least three extension reproductions plus the dossier, critique, memo, and proposal gates are the minimum finishing segment for an honest `P6-ready` claim.
- `X01-X07` beyond that deepen the `P6` floor and help a strong learner start moving toward early `P7`.
- `P8` is included only as long-horizon context.

## The P6 evidence gates

Call the outcome `P6` only if the learner can show all of the following:

- completion of the live core and the reading-and-critique layer
- at least three extension reproductions across circuits, editing or memory, and auditing
- a scoped reproduction dossier with a baseline, one constrained sweep or ablation, a failure-analysis section, and a next experiment
- one artifact or graph critique that clearly separates evidence, inference, and speculation
- one two-page memo and one short presentation that make a decision rather than only summarizing content
- one two-week proposal that is small enough to run, specific enough to fail, and clear about risks plus stop conditions
- rubric results with no dimension at "not ready" and every dimension at least "ready"

If those four gates are missing, the learner may still be in `P5` or `P5+`, but the repository should not call the result `P6`.

## What makes the P6 claim plausible for an ordinary undergraduate

- The course keeps the compute burden low enough for repetition in Colab.
- The bootcamp forces notes, logs, memos, critiques, and proposals rather than notebook completion alone.
- The repository mixes runnable modules with reading-only critique modules, which is closer to real scoped research work than pure coding or pure reading.
- The final deliverables already match the shape of an early research task: reproduce, judge, communicate, and propose the next step.

## What still comes after the repository

- More original experiments, more external feedback, and stronger problem selection are still required for `P7+`.
- One repository can credibly build `P6-ready` behavior; it cannot by itself certify long-horizon seniority.
