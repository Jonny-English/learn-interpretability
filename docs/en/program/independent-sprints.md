# Independent Research Sprints

The point of this file is to simulate task shape for students and independent researchers. Serious self-directed research is rarely "read these three articles and tell me what you learned." It is usually constrained, ambiguous, and output-driven.

## Task 1: Fast paper brief

- Timebox: 90 minutes
- Input: any article from `M03-M10`
- Required output:
  - one-page brief
  - one key figure
  - three risks or limitations
- Review questions:
  - did you identify the strongest evidence?
  - did you name the biggest weakness?
  - did you propose a next experiment rather than a vague idea?

## Task 2: Constrained reproduction

- Timebox: 2-3 hours
- Input: one runnable notebook from `M01`, `M02`, `M04`, or `M05`
- Constraint: change only one variable family
- Required output:
  - one figure
  - one table
  - one experiment log
- Review questions:
  - what stayed fixed?
  - what changed?
  - what judgment call did you make after seeing the result?

## Task 3: Adversarial artifact reading

- Timebox: 90-120 minutes
- Input: one artifact-heavy article from `M03`, `M07`, `M09`, or `M10`
- Required output:
  - one critique
  - one ambiguity list
  - one proposed supplementary experiment
- Review questions:
  - did you write what the artifact does not prove?
  - did you separate evidence from interpretation?
  - did the proposed experiment actually target the ambiguity?

## Task 4: Two-week proposal

- Timebox: half a day
- Input: one capstone direction
- Required output:
  - a two-page proposal
  - an experiment budget
  - a risk section and stop condition
- Review questions:
  - is the scope small enough to finish?
  - is there a baseline?
  - is there a real reason to stop if the signal is weak?

## Task 5: P6 gate dossier

- Timebox: 1 day
- Input: combine one constrained reproduction, one critique, and one capstone direction
- Required output:
  - one scoped research-task dossier
  - one two-page memo
  - one next experiment
- Review questions:
  - does the dossier make a real research judgment rather than only retell the course?
  - is there a baseline, one constrained change, and one failure-analysis section?
  - could a mentor or peer plausibly hand you the next scoped task after reading it?

## Recommended order

Use the tasks in this order:

1. Fast paper brief
2. Constrained reproduction
3. Adversarial artifact reading
4. Two-week proposal
5. P6 gate dossier

That order forces the same transition an early-stage researcher usually needs: understand, reproduce, critique, propose.
